Granny's Recipes

Nothing brings back memories quite like the smell of Granny's homemade pie fresh out of the oven. In a world where the internet is filled with either ultra-healthy but tasteless meals or overly complicated recipes with endless ingredients, Granny believes in something different, simple, quick and delicious.

Back in the day, no one had time for fussy techniques or expensive ingredients, yet every meal felt like a warm hug. Here, you'll find time-tested recipes that are easy to make, full of flavor, and straight from the heart.

Because good food should never be a compromise.

Let's cook !

Granny's soup

We never think of soup but we love soup! Here is the perfect sweater weather meal!

    Ingredients:
  • 1 chopped onion
  • 2 sliced carrots
  • 2 diced potatoes
  • 4 cups of vegetable/chicken broth
  • Salt, pepper and herbs like thyme or rosemary
    Instructions:
  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion until soft.
  2. Add carrots and potatoes, stir for a couple of minutes.
  3. Pour in the broth and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes, until veggies are tender.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs. Serve warm with a slice of bread.

Creamy Mustard Sauce

The one thing that can save your meal is your sauce! And mustard isn't just for hot dogs!

    Ingredients:
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or milk for a lighter version)
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard (or whole-grain mustard for extra texture)
  • 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (optional, for depth)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
    Instructions:
  1. Melt the butter in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until soft and golden.
  3. Stir in the mustard and Worcestershire sauce.
  4. Pour in the cream and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve over grilled chicken, steak, or mix with pasta for a rich, creamy dish!

Rustic Jam Tart

We all deserve a treat sometimes, and a tart is the most lovely treat in the planet!

    Ingredients:
  • 1 sheet of store-bought pie dough (or puff pastry)
  • ½ cup of your favorite jam (strawberry, apricot, raspberry, etc.)
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional, for sprinkling)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash, optional)
    Instructions:
  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie dough and place it on a baking sheet.
  3. Spread the jam in the center, leaving about 1 inch of border.
  4. Fold the edges over slightly to create a rustic crust.
  5. (Optional) Brush the edges with egg wash and sprinkle with sugar for a golden, crispy finish.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  7. Let cool slightly, then enjoy!

One-Pan Creamy Garlic Potato & Sausage Skillet

A hearty, easy meal using simple ingredients, perfect for a cozy lunch or dinner!

    Ingredients:
  • 4 medium potatoes, diced (no need to peel if washed well)
  • 2 sausages (any kind: smoked, chicken, or even leftover sausage), sliced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup milk or cream (for a creamy version)
  • ½ cup grated cheese (optional, but delicious!)
  • 1 tbsp butter or oil
  • Salt, pepper, and a pinch of dried herbs (like thyme or parsley)
    Instructions:
  1. Heat a large pan over medium heat and melt the butter.
  2. Add the diced potatoes and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and sliced sausage. Cook for another 5-7 minutes until everything is golden brown.
  4. Pour in the milk/cream and let it simmer for 2 minutes.
  5. (Optional) Sprinkle grated cheese on top and cover for 1-2 minutes until melted.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs. Serve hot!

15 min Tomato & Egg Scramble

A simple but flavorful dish perfect for breakfast, lunch, or a light dinner. Ready in no time!

    Ingredients:
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, diced (or 1/2 cup canned tomatoes)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1 tbsp butter or oil
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • ½ tsp sugar (balances the acidity of tomatoes)
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or basil, if available)
    Instructions:
  1. Heat a pan over medium heat and add butter/oil.
  2. Sauté the chopped onion for 2 minutes until soft, then add the garlic and cook for another 30 seconds.
  3. Add the tomatoes and a pinch of salt, pepper, and sugar. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. In a bowl, beat the eggs, then pour them into the pan.
  5. Gently stir and cook until the eggs are set but still soft (about 3 minutes).
  6. Sprinkle with fresh herbs and serve warm, with bread or rice!
