Become a cleaning fairy

Why spend a fortune on harsh chemicals when Granny's got the best tricks up her sleeve?
Our tried-and-true cleaning hacks will help you keep your home spotless using simple, affordable, and natural solutions.
You'll be amazed by the creativity and resourcefulness of past generations, because they knew how to make every corner shine without breaking the bank!

Let's make thing shiny !

Baking soda Everywhere !

Sprinkle baking soda on countertops, sinks, or even the bathtub, then scrub with a damp sponge. It's great for removing stains and deodorizing.
If your carpet smells like uncle Ritchie sprinkle baking soda generously on it, let it sit for 15-30 minutes, then vacuum it up to freshen up your home.

Shiny Window

Vinegar and Water for Cleaning Windows are your now best friends. Mix equal parts vinegar and water in a spray bottle. This natural solution can clean glass, mirrors, and windows without leaving streaks.
Do not forget journal paper to save your pretty shi-fons. Before microfiber cloths were common, we used crumpled up newspaper to wipe down windows and mirrors for a streak-free finish.

Lemon for Shine

To polish your furniture, mix lemon juice with olive oil to make a natural wood polish. This leaves your furniture shining and smelling fresh.
And for the the Microwave, cut a lemon in half, place it in a bowl of water, and microwave it for a few minutes. The steam loosens grime while also leaving the microwave smelling fresh like a lemon meringue, is it still your favorite stweety ?

Stains be gone !

Grease Stains: Sprinkle salt on fresh grease stains on fabric, let it absorb, and then wash it out.
For cast iron skillets, pour salt in and scrub with a damp cloth to remove residue without damaging the seasoning. And Voila !

Cornstarch for Cleaning Upholstery

To clean fabric upholstery, sprinkle cornstarch on it and let it sit for 15-20 minutes. Then, vacuum it up. It's a natural way to lift dirt and stains.

Potato tomato

Potato of course ! To clean copper, cut a raw potato in half, dip it in baking soda, and rub it on tarnished copper items to restore their shine. It works like magic!
